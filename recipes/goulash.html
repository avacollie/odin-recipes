How to make Hungarian Goulash

Description

Gulyás or “goulash” is a beloved dish from Hungary. Translating literally to “herdman’s meat”, it is commonly eaten as a hearty winter meal.

Goulash is a stew made with meat (usually beef) and vegetables.

Ingredients

  • Pork lard
  • Yellow onions
  • Hungarian sweet paprika
  • Garlic gloves
  • Red bell peppers, cut into chunks
  • Stewing beef, cut into large pieces
  • 2 diced carrots
  • Potatoes
  • Onions
  • A pinch of salt

Steps

  1. Melt the pork lard in a heavy soup pot over high heat.
  2. Cook the onions until browned.
  3. Add the beef and cook until it is starting to brown, around 10 minutes.
  4. Add the bell peppers, tomatoes, garlic and cook for another 6-8 minutes.
  5. Remove the pot from the heat and stir in paprika, salt and other desired spices.
  6. Add the carrots and potatoes. Boil on medium heat for 30 minutes.
  7. Serve with a generous amounts of Unicum and enjoy!
