How to make Tortilla de Patatas

Description

Tortilla de patatas is a beloved dish from Spain. Translating literally to “potato omelette”, it is commonly eaten as a “tapa”, or a small dish eaten with a drink.

It is important to decide how “jugoso” (runny) you would like your tortilla de patata to be. It is fashionable in some parts of Spain to have an extremely runny tortilla de patata. To begin with, we will start with a more standard version, and you can adjust to taste after some practice.

Ingredients

  • A small, deep pan
  • A spatula
  • A flat plate
  • Oil for frying
  • Potatoes
  • Eggs
  • Onions
  • A pinch of salt

Steps

  1. Chop up some potatoes into thin slices.
  2. Fry the potatoes in a pan until they are golden-brown.
  3. Chop up the onions.
  4. Simmer the onions until soft
  5. Crack 4 large eggs in a bowl and whisk.
  6. In a separate bowl, combine the onions, potatoes, the whisked eggs and add a pinch of salt.
  7. Pour the mixture into the oiled pan and wait a few minutes.
  8. When one side is hardened, flip the tortilla using a flat plate. Then, slide the uncooked part of the tortilla back onto the pan using the plate.
  9. Cook the other side of the tortilla.
  10. Serve with a “tinto de verano” (summer red wine) and enjoy!
