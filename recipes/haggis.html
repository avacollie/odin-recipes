How to make Scottish Haggis

Back

Description

Haggis is a traditional and beloved dish from Scotland. The name originates from Old English and Norse, literally means “to cut, hack into”, it is eaten only on special occasions, such as Hogmanay (New Year’s Eve in Scotland), weddings and on Burn’s Night, the day where the famous Scottish poet Robert Burns is celebrated.

Haggis has a hearty taste.

Ingredients

  • Sheep’s stomach (washed and soaked for 8 hours)
  • Sheep’s pluck (liver, heart and lungs)
  • Suet
  • Oatmeal
  • Spices to taste

Steps

  1. Boil the sheep’s pluck for 2 hours.
  2. Drain and chop.
  3. Boil the onions.
  4. Roast the oatmeal on low heat to make it crisp.
  5. Combine the onions, oatmeal and pluck into a firm mixture.
  6. Knead by hand for 5 minutes.
  7. Stuff the sheep’s stomach until about ⅔ full.
  8. Pierce the stomach several times with a small needle to prevent it bursting during cooking.
  9. Cook covered and on low heat for 3 ½ hours.
  10. Remove the stuffing from the stomach and combine with “neeps and tatties” (mashed potatoes and turnips).
  11. Serve with a generous amounts of whiskey and enjoy!
